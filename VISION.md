# Pride With Friends Vision

This is the PWF vision document which details the dream for what the event should look like and poses questions about how to make it happen.

## Prior challenges

Prior events neglected to promote the charity and causes effectively, with primarily only myself making the calls to action. Others **must have** also done the same, but for the most part streamers let the overlay do the work instead; I only know about the streams I was able to directly monitor, so could have missed it, but I also reviewed **many** VODs and don't remember much in the way of reminding people to donate, talking about the purpose of the event or the charity, or comments on the issues at hand except when prompted specifically by chatters. It was actually more common that overlays were not added, alerts were ignored or never configured, donations were unacknowledged or unread.

As noted above, streamers frequently neglected to complete the stream setup for alerts, overlays and campaign pages. When time for the event came, they chose to quietly not participate instead and would conduct a normal stream then raid into a PWF participant. This may have been the result of confusion or panic, but again, no one would ever speak up regardless of how many times I asked people to reach out. I am not speaking about tentative invitees, either; this was true of excited participants who expressed strong interest in joining, up until they had to put it on their actual streams. I feel like a bad person if I'm hounding players to get setup, but if I don't stay on them and personally, individually reach out to almost every streamer to help them prepare everything then a LOT of them will procrastinate until they choose not to participate out of shame, frustration, or feeling overwhelmed.

Input from and communication with community members, regardless of orientation, was extremely limited. It has been my experience that people only want directions and the most low-effort mode of participation possible, rather than engage with the planning. When asked, every single person deferred to my judgment at all times, aside from specifically the other organizer who had independently decided that they wanted a community event. This is not limited to PWF, but applies equally to the ~50 other events I've planned and hosted from tournaments to protests. As a result, many interested people dropped out when asked to set up their stream assets or work out a schedule to align with other players for the "with friends" element of the event. Approximately zero people offered insight, opinions or advice leading up to any of the 3 PWF events. Very, very few people ever asked for assistance with the setup. This is not a dig at those people; it is an assertion that I have to work more to engage with people and more effectively invite stronger participation.

Very little graphics work has been available, historically. In past years, I paid an artist for multiple pieces, but overlays and layouts were still sorely lacking. I am again unemployed and tragically broke so cannot afford to buy art again. I am also devastatingly lacking in artistic talent and vision myself, so must rely on others for this effort. This is a big part of the reason I put PWF on a hiatus for 2 years; I didn't have the resources and time to avail.

## Solutions

Provide more structure for people to slot into, instead of insisting that everyone self-organize. I hate telling people what to do, I hate being a "leader", I hate hierarchies, but people generally seem to expect or require that they be given clear direction rather than expected to figure things out on their own.

Build out a website with clear, legible documentation. Provide direct links that show exactly how to configure overlays, emotes, alerts, bots, etc. Add visual aids, like annotated screenshots.

Make a more inviting discussion space, and have more people reach out to the streamers to check in on them more frequently.

DIY graphics? Extract stuff from Spire 2 also?
